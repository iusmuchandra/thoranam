Thoranam · Film One · Production Package

కృష్ణవేణి Krishnaveni — shot-by-shot video generation package

Everything needed to regenerate the 2-minute animatic as photoreal AI video: a master style block, character sheets, and eight shot cards with prompts, dialogue, and continuity notes. The stills are frames from the WebGL animatic — use them as composition references, recomposed to widescreen.

How to use this package

  • Model: best results as of mid-2026: Veo (reference images + native audio), Kling 2.x (strong character reference), or Runway Gen-4 (good camera control). All examples are plain-text prompts that work across models.
  • Pipeline per shot: (1) generate one styleframe with an image model using the shot prompt + the animatic still as composition reference; (2) feed that styleframe to image-to-video with the motion/camera text; (3) generate each sub-clip listed on the card (models cap at ~5–10s); (4) use the last frame of each sub-clip as the first frame of the next for continuity.
  • Consistency: paste the STYLE block plus the relevant character blocks into every prompt, verbatim. Lock one seed per shot family where the model allows.
  • Format: generate 16:9 (2.39:1 crop in the edit), 24fps. The film's letterbox, title cards, and subtitles are applied in the edit, not generated.
  • Audio: generate video silent. Record the Telugu dialogue (or use a Telugu TTS/voice clone), then build the mix per the audio plan at the end.

Master style block — prepend to every prompt

STYLE: Photorealistic cinematic film, a South Indian period river drama set in 1950s coastal Andhra Pradesh. Shot on 35mm anamorphic, shallow depth of field, gentle film grain, natural restored-classic color grade. Warm golden sunlight, teal-green Krishna river water with real reflections, ochre sandstone gorge cliffs, dry deciduous Nallamala forest. Slow, composed camera movement. No modern objects anywhere: no motorboats, power lines, plastic, signage, life jackets, wristwatches.

Global negative prompt

NEGATIVE: modern objects, motorboat, outboard engine, power lines, plastic, text, watermark, logo, extra limbs, deformed hands, distorted face, oversaturated HDR look, video-game render, cartoon, anime

Character & prop sheets — paste when on screen

Veeranna (the grandfather)

VEERANNA: a lean, weathered Telugu boatman in his late 60s, deep brown sun-darkened skin, white stubble and a grey handlebar mustache, a loosely tied white cotton turban, bare chest with a faded red-ochre angavastram cloth over the left shoulder, white dhoti folded to the knee, barefoot, a small brass amulet on a black thread. He stands at the stern working a long bamboo punting pole with slow, practiced strokes. Kind, unhurried eyes.

Chinnu (the grandson)

CHINNU: an 8-year-old Telugu boy, bright curious eyes, short black hair, a faded indigo cotton shirt and khaki shorts, barefoot, sitting cross-legged at the bow of the boat beside a small lit brass oil lamp, one hand steadying its base.

The boat

BOAT: a traditional Andhra wooden country boat about nine meters long, dark oiled teak planking, gracefully upswept prow and stern, a painted white-and-black eye on each side of the bow, two faded red paint bands, one patched square beige cotton sail on a bamboo mast, a low thatched-palm canopy amidships, cargo of clay pots and jute grain sacks.

Edit assembly

  • Timeline: 24fps, 2.39:1 letterbox on a 16:9 master. Cut points at 0:12, 0:25, 0:40, 1:00, 1:15, 1:30, 1:50 — hard cuts everywhere except the closing fade.
  • Title (0:04) and narration cards (0:43, 1:28 "Srisailam.", 1:52 closer) in a serif face (Iowan/Palatino class); Telugu dialogue subtitles bottom-center with speaker names, exactly as in the animatic.
  • Grade: single warm LUT across the film; let the generated time-of-day do the work. Add 4–6% film grain last.
  • The running animatic (thoranam repo, film/krishnaveni.html) is the timing reference — play it side-by-side while cutting.
